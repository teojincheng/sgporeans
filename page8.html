Short Story

‘Why are you tearing up?’ Cahya asked.
‘Its my youngest son, he has changed.’ Taylor replied. She used her own hands to wipe her eyes. She continued describing how she had been conversing with her son to know more about his romantic partner.
Both of Cahya’s arms were placed on the table, she listened to Taylor with concentration. Beside Taylor sat Josephine, she was sipping tea from a cup. She nodded at different points in time during the conversation.
‘That girl is wasting my son’s time.’Taylor muttered, shaking her head. Cahya was frowning and looked at Josephine. It seemed as though Cahya wanted Josphine to speak.
‘Speaking of girls, do you remember our trip to Chinatown three days ago?’ Josephine enquired looking at Taylor and Cahya in turn. Both of them nodded. ‘Remember that mural painting we saw on the shophouse? What are your thoughts on it ?’ Josephine asked.
‘It is important that artists have creative freedom in their work. But’ Cahya paused, she placed one finger near her chin. ‘I…uh…’ Cahya seemed to be unable to gather her thoughts.
‘The artwork is great! I thought it portrayed samsui women’s contribution to Singapore’, Taylor said smiling.
‘I still think one has to be careful because the artwork may be subtly promoting undesirable behaviour or attitudes.’ Cahya stated.
